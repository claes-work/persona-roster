# Skool premium community ("KI Business Agenten"): exit, not revival

**Decision.** End the current half-alive state now. Never install another revenue-share
operator (option C) and treat a cash sale (option D) as unrealistic. Within a hard
60-day window, offer Alexander Schworwies (or another candidate) a real **ownership
transfer**; if nothing signs by day 60, execute a 90-day honorable sunset. Under every
path: annual members are made 100% whole, the 26-lesson Claude Code Masterclass is
carved out, kept by Sebastian, and productized as a one-time product sold through
YouTube. Confidence: high on this convergent core; the *structure* of the transfer
(earn-out vs. clean asset sale) remains a fork the founders must pick (see Dissent).

**Context.** Premium Skool community at $97/mo; peak 333 paying members, now 178 and
declining monthly. Skool shows $8,900 MRR but annual prepayments are already spent;
real cash ≈ $5,000/mo, plus a deferred service obligation to annual members (no
reserves were held). Founders have no drive left and have already decided
(2026-07-06, second brain) to prioritize YouTube > workshops > software with the
community on low flame. Own evidence: a 30%-revenue-share community manager (Marco
Hanczuch) previously drove the peak, then defected via incentive conflict and took
members with him — the failed prototype of the "install an operator" option. Other
community-management hires entertained but created no new revenue.

**Reasoning.**
- *Unanimous:* the audience (YouTube channel, Sebastian's trust bank), not the
  community, is the asset; the community is currently "a job that pays ~$5k/mo and
  taxes focus" (Hormozi: you get paid for what you do, returns on what you own).
  Keeping it half-alive out of sunk cost is the only illegitimate option — it burns
  founder attention and slowly drains the trust bank the sponsorship and workshop
  businesses run on.
- *Unanimous:* option C is a repeat of the Marco experiment (upside without ownership
  = mercenary structure). Option D as a cash sale fails because "candidates can't
  pay" — Hormozi reads that as the market's price signal, Chris Do additionally as a
  buyer-quality signal.
- *Unanimous:* annual members are a non-negotiable make-good (served-out access,
  successor assumption in writing, or pro-rata refund) — reputation is a one-way door
  and Sebastian's face is on this.
- *Unanimous (adopted during debate):* productize the Masterclass as a one-time
  product to the YouTube audience regardless of path; launch it BEFORE any sunset
  date so the cash gap is bridged; remaining members get lifetime access as the
  parting gift (goodwill, testimonials).
- *Hormozi (for the earn-out transfer):* seller-financed 10–20% of revenue for a
  fixed 24–36-month term, contractual assumption of the annual-member obligation,
  reversion clause, and a deliberate 90-day brand-association transfer
  (Alex → Alex+Kale → Kale playbook). B's downside is "a free option expired
  worthless"; A's downside "writes refund checks out of a €28k account."
- *Chris Do (for the clean asset sale):* royalty/profit-participation deals are only
  for well-run companies with good products; a 10–20% royalty on a declining,
  face-bound community whose irreplaceable element (the face) is being deliberately
  removed is equity-shaped risk that violates that rule. A 100%-seller-financed buyer
  has no skin in the game; monitoring an earn-out + reversion for 24–36 months
  debits scarce founder attention and "resentment = underpricing." Sell Alexander a
  *license to the content and tooling* for cash or a short fixed note — "sell him the
  content, not the congregation" — members opt in individually via one honest email.

**Dissent.** The fork stayed open after a full adversarial round:
- Hormozi HOLDS option B (structured transfer with earn-out + face transfer +
  reversion): the face-transfer objection is "a skill issue, not a law of nature"
  (Gym Launch precedent), and a warm intro without a deal donates the member
  relationships while keeping 100% of the liability.
- Chris Do HOLDS option A (sunset + productize; clean asset sale as the only open
  door): he retires "Marco 2.0" (ownership does fix the incentive problem) but holds
  that the goodwill bank doesn't transfer — "you can assign admin rights and member
  lists; you cannot assign trust" — and retention already failed 333→178 *with* the
  face present, so the earn-out is priced on hope.
- Convergent regardless: identical 60-day tripwire and identical sunset playbook as
  the floor.

**Assumptions.** Real cash ≈ $5k/mo and declining; founders will genuinely not
reinvest energy (both seats treat this as fixed input, not weakness); Alexander (or
similar) is interested but cannot pay cash; Atlas cockpit + Masterclass make
low-flame service of annual members cheap; German/EU consumer law permits novating or
serving out the annual obligations (both seats explicitly deflected on jurisdiction —
lawyer required).

**Rejected alternatives.**
- *C — keep ownership, install high-percentage operator:* unanimously rejected;
  already failed empirically (Marco), structurally a mercenary conflict.
- *D — outright cash sale:* no buyer exists at a meaningful price; pursuing one
  delays the decision.
- *Re-energize/rebuild ourselves:* contradicts the standing 2026-07-06 focus
  decision; belief is gone and members can tell (Chris Do: belief→feeling→action→result).
- *Do nothing / keep half-alive:* unanimously the most expensive option.

**Risks / next experiment.**
- Top risks: (1) earn-out rounds to zero after face removal (if B), bought "a slower
  shutdown with your name attached"; (2) refund exposure vs. €28k account (if A
  without prior product launch); (3) community was invisible retention infrastructure
  for workshop buyers; (4) jurisdiction risk on novating annual obligations.
- Cheapest validation: week 1 — quantify the annual-member liability precisely
  (count, months owed, delivery cost) and put the ownership question to Alexander
  with a hard 60-day clock; his response (cash/note vs. only-if-free) largely decides
  the Hormozi/Chris-Do fork on its own.

**Affected.** Sebastian's GmbH (community, YouTube, workshops); no roster-repo
artifacts. Hub-candidate: yes (durable business decision — second brain should pull
this on next sync).
