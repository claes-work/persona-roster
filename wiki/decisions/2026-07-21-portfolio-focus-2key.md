# Portfolio focus: one business (2Key), everything else is a function

**Decision.** The company is a **2Key company** for a committed 3–5-year season, with a
12-month "season of no" starting now. The portfolio is reframed from "four pillars" to
one business plus functions: 2Key = the asset and exit vehicle; YouTube = 2Key's
marketing department; trainings/workshops = capped cash-flow bridge ("payroll");
internal sales-team project = freeze or kill (NOT "fold" — 2Key has no sales function
per the avatar record); Skool = execute the decided exit. The Enno investor track
continues but is **sequenced and double-tested** (below) — equity is never sold to buy
personal calm. Confidence: high on the reframe (three seats independently convergent);
medium on operational details until the tripwire numbers exist. Status `proposed`:
requires BOTH founders — Florian's role in the 2Key season is a required input, not a
signature line.

**Context.** Chronic founder re-questioning spiral across 2Key / sales-team project /
YouTube / Skool; exit ambition points to a product; YouTube is the only native lead
channel but momentum is declining; Sebastian's personal burn (expensive apartment,
~€72k salary need) makes the investor option partly an anxiolytic. Prior records in
force: Skool exit (2026-07-20), 2Key deal structure €150k/10% (2026-07-20), owner-
operator avatar / no B2B motion year 1 (2026-07-20), pricing (2026-07-21). Deep
council: independent takes → cross-examination → skeptical review (verdict: rework →
findings incorporated here).

**Reasoning.**
- *Unanimous — one business.* Exit = asset sale; EV = customers × lifetime gross
  profit ÷ risk; trainings fail the risk term (founder-dependent, no recurring revenue,
  "no exit there") — Hormozi. Pressure = force ÷ area; breakthrough requires reducing
  area — Chris Do. "Real money is in products, not personal brands"; brand =
  acquisition engine, product = enterprise value — Neil Patel.
- *Unanimous — low-ticket is not the disqualifier Sebastian thinks.* Healthy unit
  economics (low churn, high margin, big avatar, founder-free delivery) beat
  high-ticket-unscalable on enterprise value (Hormozi: "I actually like the low ticket
  thing better"). The real question is 2Key's churn/margin/payback/avatar-size numbers
  — which are currently unmeasured (see Assumptions).
- *Unanimous — YouTube reframe.* Not a pillar, not a venture that must "win": its one
  KPI is **qualified 2Key leads/trials per month** (to be instrumented — first fix).
  Never stop the only lead flow ("we never stop a flow until we create another flow" —
  Hormozi; Quick Sprout pause = −30% traffic — Neil). Judging the YouTube Engine at 2–3
  videos is "judging a bridge at the first pylon." Mechanics (Neil): ~1 strong
  keyword-targeted German search video/week, ~3:1 search-to-legacy format ratio (the
  hype audience is the ignition system for the search library), email capture on every
  video + first-24h list blast (the list is the deed, the audience is a lease), ~1
  founder-day/week, 2-year patience clock — but with **numeric quarterly lead-quality
  checkpoints** so the clock can lose to a checkpoint (reviewer fix).
- *Unanimous — trainings capped, not killed.* Fixed slots, zero growth investment,
  zero new formats for 90 days; they fund founder salaries (quit-timing rule: keep
  stable income until the new vehicle matches it). Kept as a **listening post** (Chris
  Do: teaching is where clarity comes from; live rooms feed the content engine).
  **Gated webinar option** (converged Hormozi×Neil position after debate): productize
  the existing workshop into an automated evergreen funnel ONLY IF (a) 2Key agent
  launch shipped AND (b) capped live slots sell out; max 10 founder-days, existing
  material only, KPI = founder-hours freed at equal cash (not growth), live flow cut
  only as the webinar replaces it.
- *Investor double-test + sequencing (all three seats converged; reviewer sharpened):*
  1. **Sequence:** the already-decided 30-day pre-order avatar test runs BEFORE the
     pitch closes. A pass strengthens the ask; a fail means the money should not be
     taken (equity at a possibly-wrong avatar is the only irreversible act in the
     plan). Before results, write down which points of THIS record a failure reopens.
  2. **Use-of-proceeds:** ONE canonical memo must be reconciled with the deal-struktur
     record's existing allocation (50% marketing hire / 27% gated ads / brand+legal /
     buffer — which has no agent-launch line item). Proceeds fund a named constraint;
     founder salary at most a market-minimum footnote. Salary-first = "not a deal,
     that's outsourcing your discomfort — and now it has a cap table" (Hormozi);
     "a founder whose personal burn dictates strategy IS hair" (Chris Do via Neil's
     no-hair standard).
  3. **Solvency precondition:** replace-income break-even (capped trainings margin vs.
     both founders' personal numbers, 60/40 split per deal record) + runway + worry
     date, computed BEFORE the next Enno touchpoint, owned by Florian or the StB (not
     Sebastian — incentive to fudge), with "clears" defined as a number. If it doesn't
     clear: fix burn or output first; dilution never papers over personal burn.
  4. **Pitch preconditions (reviewer):** Skool annual-member liability quantified and
     disclosable (due-diligence landmine otherwise — nobody at the table had connected
     the Skool record to the pitch); StB checklist (SV-Status, vGA exposure if salary
     changes near the round) as blocking tasks.
  - Keep the track itself: "always be fundraising about a year before you need the
    cash" — the relationship is built precisely when you don't need him (Neil,
    restating Chris Do's walk-away-power point as process). One warm investor is a
    dependency, not a market (Hormozi: only-one-deal-means-no).
- *No business-adult via equity (unanimous).* "We're creative, we need a finance
  person" = a partner-shaped hole — a skill to learn, not a person to buy (Chris Do,
  3 failed partnerships; Hormozi, ~8–9; consistent with the deal record's Option-B
  rejection: services never in the cap table).
- *Apartment/€72k:* not ordered sold, but converted from an unexamined hostage-taker
  into a **chosen cost** with computed runway and worry date ("low expenses are the
  source of your walk-away power" — Chris Do; "a bigger house just means you work
  more" — Neil; to-sacrifice list — Hormozi).
- *Anti-spiral mechanism:* (1) signed two-founder commitment memo — re-read, never
  re-decide; (2) written load-bearing assumptions with **numeric** push-vs-pivot
  tripwires + horizon dates + a named non-founder reader (quarterly council review
  seat) — reviewer fix: tripwires without numbers make every bad Tuesday a debate
  about whether something was falsified; (3) ONE clock table reconciling season-of-no
  (12 mo), YouTube patience (2 yr), season (3–5 yr), Skool day-60, quarterly
  checkpoints — so a bad quarter can't trigger an argument about which clock governs;
  (4) measure inputs weekly (videos shipped, demos, churn), be patient with outputs;
  (5) belief-repair layer (Chris Do: a memo is a tactics fix; belief → feeling →
  action → result — pair it with small early wins, success measured as growth vs. own
  past numbers, public commitment to the audience; "the 'this is the path' feeling
  comes from momentum, not before commitment").

**Dissent.**
- *Premium tier timing:* Neil Patel holds that a high-price tier inside 2Key belongs
  within 12 months (Toyota/Ferrari; discounted signups churned ~29% faster; workshop
  sales = proven willingness-to-pay). Hormozi holds price-ladder-later. Reviewer
  weakens Neil's evidence base: workshop buyers are largely a different tribe than the
  owner-operator avatar ("ein anderes Tier" per the Zielgruppe record) — cheapest
  close: count how many existing workshop clients match the avatar; ≥20–30% match
  reopens the fork, near-zero closes it in Hormozi's favor.
- *Creativity outlet:* Chris Do prescribes a sanctioned 80/20 (80% committed thing,
  20% personal R&D) so creativity stops leaking into strategy. Hormozi calls this
  wrong for THESE founders ("rationing an addiction is not replacing it — 20%
  open-ended R&D is a permission slip for the spiral"); creativity lives INSIDE 2Key
  (hooks, offers, videos) in year 1, revisit in year 2. The founders must pick one —
  an anti-relitigation memo cannot ship with open forks (reviewer).
- *Memo vs. belief:* Hormozi ("feelings don't get a vote on behavior") vs. Chris Do
  ("a belief problem masquerading as a strategy problem — the groove is deeper than
  the paper; contract WITHOUT belief repair is why smart founders relapse").
  Synthesis adopted both layers; the tension is preserved because it predicts the
  failure mode: if the spiral survives the memo, the belief layer was skipped.

**Assumptions.** 2Key's unit economics are healthy enough to justify the season
(churn, gross margin, payback, avatar size — UNMEASURED at decision time; measuring
them is part of the season's first quarter, and the signed memo must state them as
falsifiable tripwires). The YouTube audience (technical, n8n-adjacent) can produce
qualified owner-operator leads at all — flagged by prior records as audience≠avatar
risk; the quarterly lead-quality checkpoint exists precisely to falsify this. Capped
trainings actually cover both founders' personal numbers (to be computed — solvency
precondition). Florian's read of his own role converges with this record (unverified —
he was not represented at this council).

**Rejected alternatives.**
- *Keep ranking four pillars / find the flawless pillar:* every path has problems;
  the grown-up question is "which problems do we prefer" (Hormozi). The thousand
  reasons against any single pillar are the terrain, not a discovery.
- *High-ticket pivot (trainings as the main business):* fails the exit arithmetic on
  the risk term; income vs. asset — "one ass, two horses."
- *Raise primarily to fund founder-salary calm:* unanimously rejected — dilution as
  anxiolytic; fails Hormozi's constraint test, Chris Do's walk-away doctrine, and
  Neil's own no-hair standard.
- *Buy a business-adult with equity:* rejected (partner-shaped hole; Option-B
  precedent).
- *Kill or pause YouTube:* rejected — only native lead flow; pause costs compound
  (Quick Sprout −30%).
- *Build the evergreen webinar now, ungated:* rejected as "a snack mid-Heimlich"
  (Chris Do) — right playbook, wrong year; survives only as the gated option above.

**Risks / next experiment.**
- Top risks: (1) equity sold before the avatar test resolves (irreversible; mitigated
  by sequencing); (2) Skool liability surfaces in due diligence undisclosed (mitigated
  by pitch precondition); (3) tripwires stay numberless → zombie commitment or spiral
  with a compliance badge; (4) Florian divergence turns the memo into the artifact the
  founders litigate; (5) audience≠avatar makes the YouTube patience clock a 2-year
  sunk cost (mitigated by numeric quarterly checkpoint).
- Cheapest validation chain (in order): quantify Skool liability (already the Skool
  record's week-1 action) → solvency computation with named owner + threshold → 30-day
  pre-order avatar test → THEN the Enno pitch with the reconciled memo. The avatar
  test result largely decides the premium-tier fork too.
