# Round 2: the creator business operates, 2Key is the only build

**Decision.** Amended verdict after Sebastian's real numbers (€20k/mo costs vs.
€20–23k/mo mixed revenue, break-even, no growth) and Enno's probable withdrawal:
**the creator/education business IS the operating business today; 2Key is the
committed, bounded BUILD inside it.** The signable commitment sentence: *"2Key is the
only thing we BUILD. Everything else we only OPERATE — capped hours, repriced, no new
formats — for 12 months."* The memo commits inputs (moratorium on new bets, tripwires,
founder-hour budgets), not outcomes — no one asks 2Key to carry €20k tomorrow.
YouTube is NOT 2Key's marketing department (Neil Patel formally retracted his round-1
framing); it is the cash engine + trust bank and its content is not pivoted. The
**free 10k Skool community + a to-be-built email list are 2Key's near-term marketing
department**, proven or falsified by a merged 90-day test. Bootstrap is now the
official financing path. Confidence: high on the reframe (3 seats convergent + both
prior rounds); blocked-on-Florian for signature. Skeptical review: ship-with-changes;
all four blocking fixes incorporated below.

**Context.** Round 1 (same day) framed 2Key as "the one business" with YouTube as its
marketing arm. Sebastian pushed back with facts: the YouTube channel is German
n8n/Claude-Code builder content — not 2Key's non-technical owner-operator avatar;
sponsorships (€2.5k/video, realistically 4–5/mo, main payer Hostinger whose VPS ads
fit the existing content) are load-bearing and would weaken under a content pivot;
paid Skool €4–5k/mo is declining with exit decided; workshops ≈ €6k/mo; the mix only
covers costs if everything continues as-is. Enno signalled withdrawal (low-price SaaS
too competitive, visibility-first). Sebastian's memo objection: "commitment is only
honest if the numbers could work — on 2Key alone they can't today."

**Reasoning.**
- *Reframe (unanimous):* "Commitment is the elimination of alternatives, not the
  elimination of income — what gets eliminated is new bets, not old euros" (Hormozi).
  "Specialize externally, generalize internally: externally you are the n8n/Claude-
  Code educators — that's what 10k members and Hostinger actually buy; 2Key earns the
  company's identity when strangers pay for it, not before. Blind funded The Futur
  for years and was never called 'a Futur company'" (Chris Do). Identity follows
  proof; the commitment stands regardless.
- *Memo objection answered (Chris Do):* Sebastian fuses a fact (2Key can't pay €20k
  today — true, and not asked) with a belief ("therefore commitment is dishonest" —
  the thousand-reasons machine; the tell is "I already know that"). The smallest
  honest commitment commits inputs. Hormozi: the memo was always about the test, the
  tripwires, and the moratorium — "fear exists in the vague"; that version is
  signable today.
- *YouTube (corrected):* channel-fits-buyer rule — the buyer is not in the room
  (Neil, retracting round 1 explicitly). Keep the mix: ~70% proven n8n/CC content
  (keeps Hostinger), ~20% adjacent, ~10% 2Key experiments (Hormozi 70/20/10). Add
  German keyword-search videos WITHIN the existing topic (YouTube-autocomplete +
  Ubersuggest long-tails, keyword in title + spoken aloud, long description, manual
  captions, first-24h email/community blast; ~1 founder-day/wk — Neil's concrete
  explanation, with 5 example titles in session output). The only 2Key bridge on
  this channel is indirect: builders who serve owner-operators (implementer/partner
  motion), not direct demand gen.
- *Free Skool 10k = the near-term 2Key channel, with two hard caveats:* (1) it is a
  RENTED audience — priority one is converting members to an owned email list (lead
  magnets, onboarding questions → email; Neil's $400k Facebook-fans lesson). Weekly
  motion without building courses: 2–3 value posts, reply to every comment, weekly
  question thread, monthly survey to validate/pre-sell, max 1 promotion/week
  (give-nine-ask-once). (2) *Reviewer blocking fix A2:* whether the avatar is even IN
  those 10k members is unverified — the community was fed by the same builder
  channel. **Week-1 avatar census** (question thread + survey: owner-operator vs.
  builder-serving-clients) BEFORE tripwire numbers are signed; <10% owner-operator
  share reclassifies the community as an implementer/partner channel and reopens the
  channel question.
- *Merged 90-day test (reviewer blocking fix A1):* Neil's "100 qualified trials/
  month" misapplied his own 1% rule (1% of 10k = 100 TOTAL) and silently downgraded
  the already-decided paid pre-order standard. Corrected tripwires: **≥50 PAID
  pre-orders within 30 days of active promotion through the community = pass**
  (Enno's visibility objection disproven); **<20 = channel problem**; qualified free
  trials tracked separately with a pass line of 30–50 CUMULATIVE in 90 days.
  Hormozi's framing holds: this is the "atomic-bomb effort" test — a real miss is a
  falsified fundamental assumption and the pivot rule fires honestly (that is the
  memo working, not failing).
- *Sequencing (reviewer blocking fix R1 — the plan must not break its own
  commitment sentence):* BUILD vs. REPACKAGE defined in the memo. Masterclass
  product = repackaging 26 existing lessons (OPERATE, time-capped; hard external
  deadline from the Skool sunset clock, and it replaces the dying €4–5k flow —
  consistent with the Skool-exit record's launch-before-sunset mandate). Workshop
  redesign = re-curriculum on existing material (OPERATE, at the next natural
  booking). Email capture second. 10% YouTube experiments deferred to month 2–3.
  Promotion-slot budget: the masterclass owns the community's ~1 promo/week until
  launch (~weeks 1–5), the 2Key pre-order test owns it after — never both cold.
  **2Key's protected weekly build hours get stated as a number in the memo.**
- *Workshops → sales channel (all three seats):* per-person billing, more seats,
  curriculum = 2Key onboarding from existing material, 1:1 swap inside the same
  calendar cap, KPI = **activated 2Key accounts per workshop** (not revenue), kill
  test after 2 runs with zero paying 2Key customers (revert; re-examine the avatar,
  not the memo). Chris Do additionally: reprice ~2× (three-yeses rule; €6k for 3
  founder-days is underpriced; resentment check) — client-financed acquisition
  (Hormozi: customers pay you to be acquired).
- *Costs (Chris Do's order of operations):* decompose the €20k into founder salaries
  vs. true overhead vs. delivery COGS ("salary is not profit — that's paying a
  normal person to do your job"; likely the largest line is private burn wearing a
  company badge); compute runway + worry date; then (1) cut before build, (2)
  reprice what already sells, (3) only then growth spend. Rule (Hormozi): expenses
  may never be the binding constraint on strategy; the one forbidden answer is
  out-earning via more founder-calendar hours — that pays rent with 2Key's oxygen.
  *Reviewer A5:* the solvency computation was already a round-1 precondition and is
  still not executed — the memo may not be signed until the decomposition exists.
- *Hostinger concentration (reviewer R2, previously unaddressed):* ~50–60% of
  revenue from one payer. Mitigations: second sponsor inside the same content truth
  (VPS/hosting/AI-tooling adjacent, no editorial pivot), push per-video deals toward
  quarterly commitments, masterclass revenue as the explicit hedge, "Hostinger
  churn" added to the tripwire table with a pre-agreed response that is not
  founder-hours.
- *Exit-thesis death date (reviewer A4):* the amended identity framing must not let
  the exit goal die silently. Memo sentence: **at month 12, 2Key MRR ≥ a named
  threshold (the ~€7.5k B2B-gate figure from the Zielgruppe record is available) OR
  the exit thesis is formally re-decided in council.** Also resolved the Enno
  inconsistency: his pass is filed as n=1 information, but his *diagnosis*
  (visibility-first) is adopted as the plan's premise — adopted on the merits, not
  on his authority.
- *One clock table (reviewer R4):* 90-day community test, 2-run workshop kill test,
  12-month operate cap, Skool day-60 sunset, month-12 exit-thesis gate — all
  reconciled into the single table from round 1, updated in the same commit as the
  memo.

**Dissent.** None on the reframe — genuinely unanimous, including Neil Patel's
explicit retraction of his own round-1 framing. Round-1 forks remain open where not
resolved here: 80/20 creativity outlet vs. creativity-inside-2Key (must be closed at
memo signing); premium-tier timing (avatar census + pre-order test will largely
decide it). The round-1 record's structural core (trainings capped, sales project
dead, Skool exit, no business-adult via equity, anti-spiral mechanism) remains in
force unamended.

**Assumptions.** The free community contains a meaningful share of the owner-operator
avatar (UNVERIFIED — week-1 census is the gate); Hostinger holds through the season
(hedged, not assumed); the €20k decomposition confirms founder salaries as the
dominant line (Chris Do's bet — to be verified before signature); Florian converges
(STILL UNREPRESENTED after two rounds — signature hard-gated on his session or
written delegation; reviewer marks this blocking).

**Rejected alternatives.**
- *Pivot the YouTube channel to 2Key content:* trades a proven €10–12.5k flow for an
  unproven one; audience is the wrong room; killed unanimously.
- *"2Key is the company" as external identity today:* brands are built from truth;
  positioning follows externally-quantified proof (Chris Do).
- *Relying on the raise:* Enno's withdrawal makes bootstrap official; it was the
  default anyway ("it WAS the plan" — Chris Do).
- *Out-earning the private burn via more founder-calendar hours:* the forbidden
  answer (all seats).
- *100-trials/month tripwire:* rejected by review as ~10× inflated and a silent
  downgrade from the paid pre-order standard.

**Risks / next experiment.** Top risks: (1) three parallel repackage-projects
relapse into the parallel-build disease (mitigated by sequencing + protected 2Key
build hours); (2) Hostinger churn hole ≈ €10k/mo (mitigated above); (3) avatar not
in the community → both channels fail Enno's objection → early honest pivot (that is
the mechanism working); (4) memo signed without Florian → the litigable artifact.
Next experiment chain (order): €20k decomposition + runway/worry date (owner:
Florian/StB) → week-1 avatar census → masterclass launch (owns promo slots) → 30-day
paid pre-order test through the community → month-3 checkpoint against the clock
table.
