# Round 4: free tier stays, card follows value; one domain, funnel first

**Decision.** (1) The **75-credit Starter tier is NOT removed and NOT replaced by a
card-upfront time trial** — unanimous. It IS the test version Sebastian wants:
usage-based, expiring exactly at the peak-pain moment the pricing record built the
one-click checkout around. Signup stays email-only, no card; **the card appears at
the 100%-consumed popup and is charged at that moment** — "charge at desire, never
at calendar" (Hormozi). (2) Card-entry is NOT the end of the funnel — it is the
middle; the funnel ends at the retained, ascended, referring user. (3) Website:
consolidating the scattered mini-sites and YouTube companion articles onto ONE
domain is approved as a legitimate positioning/SEO move — but as a **phased
migration into the round-3 MVP shell** (homepage + offer pages + /artikel section,
off-the-shelf commerce, still no self-built shop), with **2Key funnel pages
shipping first** and article migration rolling behind on capped OPERATE hours.
Confidence: high.

**Reasoning.**
- *Usage beats calendar (unanimous):* a time trial converts at an arbitrary
  calendar cliff — day 14 arrives whether the user activated or not, charging the
  wrong people and expiring the right ones mid-discovery (Hormozi). The credit
  meter converts at proven value: 100% consumed = activated + wants more, and the
  user blames themselves, not the product, when it runs out (usage-pricing
  psychology). The aha (first dictation <10 min) and the pain (credits gone) are
  usage events; "the 75-credit wall IS your trial expiry, and it fires exactly at
  peak pain" (Neil).
- *Card-before-value rejected as default (unanimous):* pre-flow friction strangles
  volume when the problem is no flow yet — "Get Flow. Monetize Flow. THEN Add
  Friction" (Hormozi); auto-convert-at-day-X manufactures forgot-to-cancel
  revenue → refunds and resentment ("a refund is only an apology"). For a
  **data-sovereignty brand** selling to skeptical German owner-operators, demanding
  the most sensitive payment data before any value is the brand contradicting
  itself in the first ten seconds — "a card demand before value is a toll booth at
  the front door", and removing the free tier would break the Matchmaker promise
  ("stay free if it's enough") that makes the non-needy posture credible
  (Chris Do). Freemium's job is mindshare and lead flow, not revenue — Neil's own
  pattern (Ubersuggest at ~$200k/mo loss; response to competition was MORE free,
  not less; Slack ~20–25% free→paid via Meeker 2019 as the only corpus number,
  secondhand).
- *End of funnel corrected (unanimous):* activation → credits consumed → paid
  conversion at the popup → earned founding-annual (anchored above monthly so
  monthly looks easy) → expansion/referral. Chris Do: the ladder is strangers →
  friends → customers → **advocates**; the scaffolded referral ask comes while
  feelings are good. Neil: measure activation, trial→paid, expansion,
  abandoned-checkout recovery — full-funnel revenue, never signup counts.
- *Agreed signup flow:* email-only signup (owned asset captured first) →
  onboarding rails to first dictation <10 min → visible credit meter, triggers at
  50 / 80–90 / 100% → peak-pain popup with one-click purchase (card here, charged
  now; top-up decoy beside monthly) → founding-annual as earned offer to active
  users and to the pre-order list at launch (deadline-bound, price locked before
  the agent-launch raise) → Matchmaker-Downsell stays live (free-forever users =
  referral engine). Pre-order window unchanged: founding annual, card charged
  immediately — that remains the ≥50 tripwire currency.
- *Website/SEO (Neil's lane):* scattered mini-sites split link equity and brand
  signals; Neil's own kissmetrics.com → neilpatel.com fold-in is the precedent.
  Cautions carried into the plan: 301s only transfer what's real (his $100k
  redirected domain "did nothing") — page-by-page mapping for pages with actual
  traffic, delete-and-301 the stale rest; optimize-over-create = revenue pages
  first. Chris Do: consolidation is a genuine positioning fix (one claim, one
  gate, one domain) but "you don't major in the minors" — the 50-pre-order
  tripwire is the year's load-bearing test; funnel in days, migration rolling
  behind.

**Dissent (minor, preserved).** Residual role of a card-upfront trial after
launch: Chris Do — none at all (it re-imports the expiry logic his seat argued out
of the pricing record); Hormozi — only as a downsell INSIDE the pre-order window,
redundant once Starter is live; Neil — keep as a middle rung offered on warm paths
only (workshop attendees, pre-order list, 80%-trigger email), never the default
public path. Shared core all three sign: never the default entry, never on the
public pricing page. Open for the A/B backlog alongside Hormozi's credit-split
hypothesis.

**Assumptions.** Meter + triggers + one-click checkout actually ship (they are the
mechanism this ruling leans on — still the highest-ROI dev work per the pricing
record); companion articles have measurable traffic worth 301ing (check Search
Console before migrating; delete-and-301 the rest).

**Rejected alternatives.** Removing the free tier (breaks Matchmaker + mindshare +
the only stranger path); card-upfront as default entry (trust contradiction,
wrong-stage friction); auto-convert calendar trials (forgot-to-cancel revenue);
treating article consolidation as license for the full self-built rebuild (round-3
denial stands).

**Risks / next experiment.** Risk: free tier without expiry converts slowly —
already covered by the pricing record's <2–3% A/B trigger. Next steps in order:
ship popup checkout + triggers (pricing record's Prio 1, unchanged) → 2Key funnel
pages on the one domain → /artikel section with mapped 301s.
