# Round 6: promotion map, email consent strategy — structure CLOSED

**Decision.** (1) **Emailing the ~11k member addresses without marketing consent is
banned** — unanimous, on brand and marketing doctrine before any legal question
("the data-sovereignty company pricing unsolicited use of 11,000 people's data at
€300 a complaint" — Chris Do; "the fine is the cheapest item on that invoice" —
Neil: cold-blast poisons the sending domain for the consented list they're about
to build, burns brand, produces a junk list). (2) **Consented external list =
the spine; the Skool 72h post-email = the bridge and an asset** (arrives where the
relationship lives; each qualifying post is written AS an email and drives one
consent moment — quiz, masterclass registration, lead magnet = the double-opt-in
harvest; non-consent members get 72h posts until they self-convert). First action:
**count the consent segment.** (3) Frequency allocation fixed (table below).
(4) Website↔Skool flows confirmed; one flow removed: **no backward edges**.
(5) **The architecture is declared CLOSED** — it re-opens only on a falsified
written tripwire, never on a wobbly Tuesday. Confidence: high (unanimous).

**Frequency allocation (Neil; the in-video cap is Hostinger-aware).**

| Asset | In-video | Pin | Description | 72h Skool post-email | External email (consent) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Community | verbal CTA every video | always (phase hook varies) | slot 1 always | — | footer link |
| Workshop | ad segment ~1 in 4, topic-matched | only workshop-adjacent videos | slot 2 always | ~1 ask-post / 2 weeks | 1 in ~3 sends |
| 2Key | phase 2 every video; evergreen ~1 in 4 | pre-order-window peaks only | slot 3 always | phase 2 weekly ask-post | phase-2 sequence, then 1 in ~3 |
| Courses | never on YouTube (sold inside community; pin carries the community with masterclass HOOK during launch, not a course link) | no | no | ~1 ask-post / week | launch sequences + triggers |

**In-video self-ads:** max-2-asks governs the WHOLE video — Hostinger's paid slot
is ask #1, so exactly ONE rotating self-ad segment per video (workshop → 2Key →
community deep-pitch), never stacked behind the sponsor read.

**Consolidated structure (Chris Do, "in one breath" — definitive).** YouTube turns
strangers into community members (the ONLY Skool growth engine; the stall-worry is
moot — no ruling ever dropped those links). The free community turns viewers into
friends (9:1 value rhythm, consent harvesting, home of the classroom). The
classroom sells the two masterclass courses one-time; **Schnupper previews are
fine and change nothing** — free excerpts proving value are give-first inside the
room, keep them excerpts. The website is filter + claim — all five pages stand; it
does not *advertise* Skool as an offer, it *routes* to it as the left door
("growth is YouTube's job, the site's job is qualification"). The workshop is
high-ticket paid discovery, booked only via the site. 2Key sits at the top of the
ladder, sold only to the warmed — waitlist now, pre-order in the window, masthead
after the tripwire.

**Dissent.** None. (Both seats explicitly deflected on German law specifics —
UWG/GDPR scope of Skool-collected addresses remains a lawyer question, but the
council's ban on option (c) makes it operationally moot; the open legal check is
only whether the CONSENT subset's opt-ins cover external marketing mail.)

**Assumptions.** Skool's 72h post-email reliably reaches members' inboxes; the
consent segment is measurable in the existing tooling; Hostinger contract tolerates
one self-ad segment per video.

**Rejected alternatives.** Option (c) bulk email without consent (banned — brand
suicide + deliverability poison + junk list; the €300–400 framing prices the wrong
cost); dropping YouTube→Skool links (community growth would stall — confirmed
compounding-flow logic); backward links from 2Key/classroom surfaces (loops leak
conversions and muddy unique-link attribution); more than one self-ad segment per
video.

**Risks / next experiment.** Risk: consent segment turns out tiny → the 72h
post-email carries the whole phase-1 load (it can: 72h ≈ the 2–3/week cadence).
Next actions in order: count the consent segment → set up the three 72h post
formats (value teach / question thread / weekly ask with one consent CTA) → wire
the unique-link set → build the five pages.

**Affected.** YouTube video/description/pin templates, Skool post cadence, email
tooling (consent segmentation, tags), website (unchanged from round 5), workshop
booking flow. Completes the round-5 record. Hub-candidate: yes.
