# Round 5: website IA, funnel order, and YouTube placement

**Decision.** New fact accepted: **no on-site commerce at all** (YouTube shop
integration only supports approved providers → direct website purchasing dropped;
courses sell ONLY in the free Skool classroom). The website becomes a pure
filter-and-routing layer. Five-page site map, one job + one CTA per page; the funnel
is ONE ladder with a two-buyer gate; the pinned comment carries exactly one
phase-dependent campaign link; measurement runs on unique links per placement +
onboarding attribution + monthly bank reconciliation. Hormozi's offer hierarchy from
rounds 3–4 unchanged. Confidence: high (two-seat convergence on structure).
**Moderator correction:** Chris Do's closing soft offer re-included "workshop fee
creditable on pre-order" — that mechanic was killed in round 3 (reviewer F1,
accepted: a discount in credit costume, violates the no-Rabatte rule). It stays
dead; the workshop's closing offer is the visible menu add-on / plain pre-order.

**Site map (Chris Do).**
- **Homepage** — stake the one claim ("the Claude-Code/AI-automation educators"),
  split the gate: "Selber bauen lernen" → Community · "Nicht tippen, diktieren"
  (2Key worded as what exists) → /2key. One paragraph, proof, two doors, no third.
- **/workshop** — qualify + book: proof/case style, who it's for, **price bracket
  published** (price as filter), CTA = booking inquiry (the site's only buy motion,
  inquiry-shaped as fits high-ticket).
- **/2key** — felt pain → waitlist (pre-order CTA during days 45–90), nothing else.
- **/community** — move strangers into the free Skool; the courses are presented
  HERE as classroom contents ("what's inside"), CTA = join free.
- **Email capture as the spine, not a page** — free resource + newsletter box on
  the homepage and (phase 2) every /artikel page. List = owned; Skool = rented.

**Course-pages fork (preserved, small).** Chris Do: no standalone course pages —
a product page whose buy button lives on another platform confuses ("send nothing
that could confuse"); present courses only as community contents (constraint
becomes a join-reason). Neil: standalone course pages as **SEO landing surfaces**
(searchable German long-tails) with exactly ONE button — "Diesen Kurs bekommst du
im Classroom unserer kostenlosen Community → Jetzt kostenlos beitreten" — the
community join IS the page's conversion event, never a dead end. Reconciliation
note (moderator, structural): Neil's version satisfies Chris Do's one-job-one-CTA
rule because its conversion event is the join, not a remote purchase. Practical
order: launch without course pages (Chris Do minimum); add Neil's SEO course pages
in phase 2 alongside /artikel.

**Funnel order (Neil, numbered; Chris Do concurs structurally).**
1. Video — value/trust → verbal CTA to community
2. Pinned comment — ONE phase-priority link
3. Description — the ladder in order (community first, then workshop, then
   [phase-dependent 2Key], sponsor per contract)
4. Homepage — claim + gate → booking page / 2key
5. (Phase 2) course pages — SEO capture → community join
6. Community (10k) — census + 9:1 nurture → email list (quiz/magnets)
7. Email list — 2–3/wk owned nurture, monthly scrub → phase offer
8. Classroom — course revenue → workshop upsell
9. Workshop — high-ticket cash, hottest leads → 2Key (menu add-on / pre-order)
10. /2key — pre-order → free 75 credits → peak-pain paid → founding-annual anchor
Every asset: one job, one forward edge. Builder path ends at courses (don't force
builders further); owner-operator path runs content → workshop → 2Key. 2Key never
sells cold (lead-temperature rule).

**Pinned comment (converged, phase-dependent).** Default + phase 1 (days 1–45):
free community, masterclass-hooked (masterclass owns the promo slots). Phase 2
(days 45–90): the campaign is the pre-order — Chris Do pins the 2Key pre-order
directly; Neil keeps the pin on the community with pre-order hook (pre-orders run
through the community; direct 2Key link goes to description slot 2 as hop-saver
for the buy-ready minority). Shared core: ONE campaign in the pin, the description
carries the stable ladder, the verbal in-video CTA names the phase priority.
Phase 3 (evergreen): back to community permanently; message-match per video
(workshop-adjacent topics may pin the booking page). Neil's honesty note: no
corpus data on pin-vs-description performance — allocation follows doctrine
(max-2-asks, platforms-are-selfish, message-match), not measured splits.

**Measurement (Neil).** Unique short-links/UTMs per placement (pin, each
description slot, each page); onboarding question "Wo kommst du her?" piped to
email as attribution layer (survives the Skool black box); email tags per lead
magnet (quiz / masterclass / content upgrade); monthly reconciliation of course
sales + pre-orders against tags and the BANK, never platform dashboards.

**Dissent.** Course pages (above, preserved). Pin during pre-order window
(Chris Do direct vs. Neil community-hooked — decide by where checkout friction is
lower when the window opens).

**Assumptions.** Skool classroom checkout is acceptable friction for course
buyers; YouTube tolerates the outbound link pattern (concentrated, not scattered);
the workshop booking inquiry flow exists or is off-the-shelf.

**Rejected alternatives.** On-site shop (platform constraint + round-3 denial);
third door on the homepage; standalone course pages at launch (deferred, not
killed); scattering many equal-weight links (max-2-asks); fee-credit revival
(stays dead per round 3).

**Risks / next experiment.** Multi-hop funnel (video → community → classroom)
costs conversion at every hop — accepted deliberately for list/census building;
the phase-2 description hop-saver is the pressure valve. Next artifact: the
day-1–90 calendar + this site map as build checklist (belongs in /plan alongside
the memo).

**Affected.** Company website (5 pages), YouTube description/pin templates, Skool
community setup, email tooling (tags, Zapier pipe), UTM link set. Refines the
round-3/4 records; Hormozi rulings untouched. Hub-candidate: yes.
