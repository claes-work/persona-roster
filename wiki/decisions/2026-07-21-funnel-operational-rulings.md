# Round 3: funnel & offer operational rulings (unanimous)

**Decision.** Operational layer under the round-2 architecture, all three seats
unanimous, skeptical review ship-with-changes (fixes F1–F5 incorporated):
**no second community — pure owned email list**; general-value content 9:1 with one
standing soft CTA; ONE workshop line (Claude-Code-only) with 2Key inside it plus a
visible menu add-on; **complete self-built website rebuild DENIED** — minimum viable
funnel instead; **paid pre-orders before free trials** ("as many trials as possible"
rejected as vanity); Sebastian's cleanup items (classroom simplification, paid-
community close, YouTube-Shop links) approved as OPERATE work. Confidence: high
(unanimous + consistent with all in-force records after fixes).

**Rulings.**
1. *List, not a second community* (unanimous): a new community is a second rented
   audience, a build, and split engagement (Neil); an operating commitment that
   recreates the Skool care-debt (Chris Do: "absent your constant narrative, members
   invent one"). Segment the LIST by quiz answers. An avatar-exchange room may exist
   later, funded by proof: 50+ paying customers asking for it.
2. *Transfer campaign 10k → email, 30 days:* pinned post + group-description/cover
   CTA; 2–3 posts/week rotating three hooks — quiz (7–13 questions, +280% vs. plain
   forms; the quiz IS the avatar census), masterclass registration (owns the promo
   slots until launch), content upgrade. Masterclass deadline = real countdown.
   List cadence 2–3/week; **dead = no CLICKS across ~1 month (7–10 sends)**
   (reviewer: opens are inflated by Apple MPP); monthly scrub; one re-permission
   email then remove; non-joiners stay in the community, capture re-run quarterly
   with fresh magnets. Rule of seven: one ignored email means nothing.
3. *Content mix:* ~9:1 general Claude-Code/AI-automation value to ask; soft CTA
   (same one link) after the hook, never hard-sell mid-body; the quiz measures, not
   the feed. 2Key-only content would pitch builders on an owner-operator product and
   burn the goodwill bank.
4. *Workshop architecture:* ONE workshop line, Claude-Code-only (n8n dropped from
   live delivery; n8n course remains a sold asset). "Curriculum = 2Key onboarding
   from existing material" means: same slides, same exercises — the hands-on parts
   are done IN 2Key, so attendees leave with an activated account as a byproduct.
   "1:1 swap in the same calendar cap" means: fixed ceiling of workshop days/month
   (~3); every new-format day replaces an old-format day, never adds one.
   **Annual-sub bundling rejected — menu instead** (Hormozi: "bundled money is mute
   money"): workshop €X or workshop + 2Key founding annual at visible €X+Y.
   *Reviewer F1:* the fee-creditable-on-pre-order option is KILLED (a discount in
   credit costume; violates the pricing record's no-Rabatte rule and muddies the
   signal). *Reviewer F2:* add-on buyers count toward the ≥50 tripwire ONLY if they
   match the avatar quiz; both numbers reported separately. 2-run kill test
   unchanged (zero paying 2Key conversions → revert, interrogate the avatar).
5. *Sebastian's plan audit:* classroom simplification + one-time course sales =
   OPERATE, approved ("subtraction plus monetizing existing assets"; a course sale
   creates no community debt). Paid-community close + YouTube-link cleanup =
   OPERATE (execution of the decided exit). YouTube Shop = link surface only, not a
   channel (Neil deflected: no corpus evidence on YouTube Shopping).
   **Complete self-built website rebuild = DENIED as scoped** (unanimous): "a
   second software product wearing a website costume" (Chris Do), "the textbook
   wrong-lever trap — a reasonable-looking problem nobody will blame you for
   solving" (Hormozi). MVP instead, off-the-shelf tooling, days not weeks: one
   repositioned homepage staking the Claude-Code/AI-automation-educator claim
   (round-2 identity), immediately splitting two paths — "learn to build it
   yourself" (courses/workshop) and the 2Key path, **worded as what exists today
   ("stop typing, dictate"), NOT "have it run for you"** (*reviewer F5:* pre-agent-
   launch that sells the phantom the pricing record banned and gestures at
   done-for-you, which the Zielgruppe record forbids); one page per offer; Neil's
   landing-page bar: fast (~+6%/second), message-matched, objections harvested from
   a community survey, simple form, exit-intent (desktop), abandoned-cart sequence.
   2Key takes the masthead only after the tripwire fires. The cathedral website
   goes in the NEXT season's memo if still wanted.
   *Reviewer F4:* ONE course catalog (asset, name, price) must be published first —
   the 26-lesson CC masterclass (Skool carve-out) vs. "Claude Code + n8n courses"
   must not become one asset with two names/prices; the Skool lifetime-access
   cohort is fenced out of the paid funnel.
6. *Sequencing (reviewer F3 disambiguated):* "pre-orders first" = OFFER HIERARCHY,
   not calendar-first. Calendar: ~days 1–45 transfer campaign + masterclass launch
   (Skool sunset clock ≈ 2026-09-18 forces this anyway); ~days 45–90 the 30-day
   paid pre-order window on the now-built list. Offer stack inside the window:
   founding annual (card charged, price locked before the agent-launch raise) →
   downsell monthly → trial WITH card → free trial without card only as last
   resort. Free-trial volume is a vanity metric ("most leads are crap; 10 qualified
   beat 200 junk"). Trial design: 30 days, 2–3 activation milestones with
   triggered emails; pre-order buyers become the launch waitlist. Exact
   trial-length/CC-upfront benchmarks: both Hormozi and Neil explicitly deflected
   (no wiki numbers) — test, don't assume.

**Dissent.** None — genuinely unanimous round; reviewer findings were scope/
consistency fixes, all adopted.

**Assumptions.** Quiz participation is high enough to make the census readable
(fallback: direct survey); the masterclass and transfer campaign can share ~2–3
promo posts/week without list fatigue (watch unsubscribe rate); off-the-shelf
checkout covers course + pre-order + trial flows without custom build.

**Rejected alternatives.** Second community (rented audience, care-debt, build);
hidden annual bundling (mute money); fee-credit on pre-orders (discount in
disguise); "as many free trials as possible" (vanity); complete self-built website
rebuild now (BUILD violation); separate 2Key workshop (spiral wearing a lanyard);
n8n live workshops (sell only what you use and stand behind).

**Risks / next experiment.** Over-packed 90-day calendar remains the top risk —
the day-1–90 calendar with owners is the next artifact (belongs in /plan); second:
avatar share in the community unknown until the quiz runs (census gate from round
2 unchanged); third: list-building from a cold start could undershoot the
pre-order window's reachable audience — masterclass registration doubles as the
mitigation. Cheapest next step: publish the one-page course catalog + quiz draft,
then the 90-day calendar.

website MVP, workshops, courses/masterclass, 2Key pre-order funnel). Refines

Zielgruppe, and pricing records after fixes. Hub-candidate: yes.
